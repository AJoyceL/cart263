## Project 1 response / Task 5

1. [Norah & Leah](https://norahwilcox.github.io/CART263/projects/project-1/Project1/)

One of the first things that stood out to me about Norah and Leah’s project was the visual design. It looked similar to the old Windows interface, which I found interesting because it made the project playfully ironic for a modern feeling project to have an old design interface. The use of the camera capture was also engaging. Being able to access my laptop’s camera and apply filters in real time made the interactive experience fun. I especially liked the wide selection of filters, and how easy it was to switch between them to see a different effect.

Another feature I found compelling was the ability to record and save multiple videos in a separate window. It was surprising that I could store several recordings and allowed to save them directly to my laptop. It’s something I’m thinking of applying to my own project. 

From a user perspective, it felt very clean and simple to navigate, which made exploring it easy. Likewise, looking at the code, I also noticed how organised it was. Even though it was long, it was clearly divided into sections, making it easier to read and understand.


2. [Sean](https://seangregoryv8.github.io/cart263/projects/1/)

The second project was interesting because of Sean’s concept of a sandbox where players are given the tools and the freedom to experiment and see what they can create or experience. I like it because it gave users the power to take incentive and let them figure things out on their own. It made my experience feel more open-minded and playful rather than strictly guided like other interactive projects.

At the same time, I found parts of it a bit confusing. While the interface is siple enough,it can be hard to immediately grasp at what you’re supposed to do or where to start when there are so many possibilities. For someone like me, who isn’t the fastest at figuring things out, it took me some time to understand. However, it felt like a part of the design, since sandboxes encourage exploration and experimentation. 

Both the design  and code felt very experimental, especially when it seemed very complex to me, but I could still recognise some familiar elements from things we’ve learned.


3. [Ya Xuan, Nerly & Ashmita](https://yaxuanpang.github.io/cart263/topics/project1/)

The third project was simple and easy to understand, which made it extremely enjoyable to explore right away. I loved how it reminded me of old arcade games. At the same time, Ya Xuan, Nerly and Ashmitha created a variation of a snake game, these changes made the experience feel fresh rather than just nostalgic. Those small differences helped make the game feel so much mor engaging to me.

One thing I especially liked was how their code was organised. It used multiple JavaScripts files instead of putting everything into a large file (compared to the previous two projects). This made the structure easier to read and navigate. Separating the code in this manner stops it from feeling overwhelming or cluttered. I found it helpful when trying to understand how everything works.

Overall, the game felt smooth. It was familiar, almost intuitive, while the adaptation and clean structure showed a good balance between creativity and organisation.
